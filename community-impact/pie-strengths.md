## Person-in-Environment Assessment
This module looks at public policy choices through a human lens. It maps out how changes in structural rules impact the daily routines of individuals and families, while actively highlighting the existing strengths of our neighborhoods.

---

### Environmental Ingress and Impact Mapping

#### Structural Barriers in the Immediate Environment
*   **Systemic Pressures on Daily Life:** AI to evaluate how the administrative rules or resource shifts logged in the context ledger create pressure points for households, such as increased travel times, complex paperwork, or reduced service hours
*   **Impact on Vulnerable Local Systems:** AI to identify where public system adjustments create gaps for families with minimal baseline resources

#### Existing Community Capacity and Strengths
*   **Informal Support Networks:** AI to identify community-led resilience patterns, such as neighborhood sharing networks, localized knowledge systems, or mutual assistance traditions
*   **Local Resident Assets:** Highlight the baseline skills, historical resilience, and organizational capacities that already exist within the community to navigate public bottlenecks
